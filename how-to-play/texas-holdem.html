The best 5 card hand can be made using any of the 7 cards - 5 community cards, and 2 of your own hole cards.

How a hand is played out (Stages of card distribution and betting)

(Example hand shown here is with 3 players)

The above figure shows the final state of cards. But in reality, the game starts with very few cards open. They are opened in various stages. They are as follows:

Stage 0 - Blinds and Antes

All mandatory bets are made. For more details, see Poker Betting Style.

2 Hole Cards are dealt to each player. The cards are dealt one at a time, clockwise, starting from the player to the left of the dealer button.

Mandatory bets placed, Hole Cards Dealt

Stage 1 - Pre-flop Betting Round

A round of betting happens as per Poker Betting Rules. In this round, players can only see their own hole cards. Based on the strength of those (or not üòâ), they make bets.

Stage 2 - Flop Cards are opened

3 community cards are opened - called the Flop cards.

Stage 3 - Flop Betting Round

Another round of betting happens. This time a lot more information is available on the strength of your hand.

Stage 4 - Turn Card is opened

1 more community card is opened - called the Turn card.

Stage 5 - Turn Betting Round

Another round of betting happens.

Stage 6 - River Card is opened

1 more community card is opened - called the River card.

Stage 7 - River Betting Round

The final round of betting takes place. Now all the community cards are open. The best 5 card hand out of any of the 7 cards that you can see will compete against other players' best 5 card hands.

Stage 8 - Showdown

Players who are still in play, show their hands.
In this case, Player 1 and 2 are vying for the pot, whereas Player 3 has folded.
