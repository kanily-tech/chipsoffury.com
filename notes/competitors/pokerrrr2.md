## Pokerrrr 2

### 1. Quick Facts
- **Marketing URL**: https://www.pokerrrrapp.com[1]
- **Developer**: Mondraw Limited (previously PT.farm Inc.)[2]
- **Platforms Supported**: iOS (9.0+), Android (4.1+); No native desktop or web version (desktop play requires Android emulators like BlueStacks, LDPlayer, or MuMuPlayer)[3][4][5]
- **Platform Type**: Mobile-only app for casual and club-style private games[6]
- **Best For**: Quick "friends games" and light-to-moderate club setups with a strong mobile focus[7]
- **Cost to Host**: Free to download; hosting games costs gold coins (in-app currency). First 20 minutes of private games are free, then 9 gold coins per hour (~$1/hour at most expensive rate). Can earn 1 free gold coin every 24 hours and get initial gold coins for free upon registration[8][9][10]
- **Registration Required**: Yes, but guest play available (Facebook login provides bonus gold and multi-device access; must be 18+ or 13+ with parental consent)[11][12]
- **Latest Version**: 7.5.9 (released November 27, 2025)[13]

### 2. Core Features
- **Game Variants**: No Limit Hold'em, Pot-Limit Omaha (PLO), Open Face Chinese Poker (OFC), 7 Card Stud, Blackjack, Gin Rummy, Rummy, and R.O.E. (Round of Each) for up to 9 players[14][15]
- **Tournament Options**: Ultrasonic SNG Tournament, Multi-Table Tournament (MTT), Lucky Dip Tournament, and tournament timer[16][17]
- **Instant Private Tables**: Invitation codes allow friends to join quickly from their phones[18]
- **Club System**: Create free private clubs for recurring groups with multiple tables and scheduled events[19][20]
- **Social Features**: In-app chat, emojis, real-life poker gestures (swipe, flick, tap), customizable poker rooms, and agent theme with poker missions and medals[21][22]
- **Profile Sharing**: Build and showcase your poker journey with medals, collections, and ranks[23]
- **Recent Updates (Nov 2025)**: Double Board feature for twice the action, Whiteout Finale event with prizes[24]

### 3. Pricing & Monetization
- **Free to Download**: Available on iOS App Store and Google Play Store[25][26]
- **Initial Free Credits**: New users receive 10,000 practice chips and initial gold coins upon registration[27][28]
- **Gold Coin System**: In-app currency used for hosting games, buying chips, accessing game history, and customizing tables (nicknames, card decks)[29][30]
- **Gold Coin Pricing**: $4 for ~18 gold coins; $130 for ~840 gold coins[31]
- **Gold Coin Uses**:
  - 1 gold coin = 1,000 chips; 600 gold coins = 840,000 chips[32]
  - Hosting costs: First 20 minutes free, then 9 gold coins per hour for private games (~$1/hour maximum)[33]
  - Tournament hosting: Free option available or purchase premium tournaments[34]
- **Earning Free Gold**: Complete actions like joining first club, Facebook login, or daily login (1 gold coin per 24 hours)[35][36]
- **Rake Capability**: App includes rake functionality for clubs[37]
- **Important Note**: Gold coins have no real-world value; app does not offer real-money gambling[38][39]

### 4. Pros
- **Easy Onboarding**: Join via short invitation codes, simple UI, and mobile-first design suitable for casual groups[40][41]
- **Wide Variety of Games**: Supports 8+ poker variants including Hold'em, Omaha, OFC, Stud, and even Blackjack and Rummy[42]
- **Flexible Play Options**: Supports one-off friend games and persistent club environments with multiple tables and scheduled events[43][44]
- **No Geo-Restrictions**: Available worldwide with no location-based restrictions[45]
- **High User Rating**: 4.8+ rating with 1M+ downloads[46]
- **Regular Updates**: Active development with new features and events added regularly[47]
- **Social Experience**: Rich mobile UI with built-in chat, emojis, gestures, and club features[48]
- **Data Privacy**: Minimal third-party tools by design - no HUDs or trackers[49]

### 5. Cons
- **Mobile Only**: No native desktop client or web browser version; desktop play requires Android emulators which can be cumbersome[50][51]
- **Hosting Costs**: While first 20 minutes are free, extended play requires purchasing gold coins (up to ~$1/hour)[52]
- **Casual Interface**: Interface, animations, and social features prioritize casual play over technical/professional tools[53]
- **Limited Security Measures**: No IP/GPS safeguards at platform level; app doesn't prohibit collusion, soft playing, or botting in terms of use[54][55]
- **Club-Dependent Security**: Safety and game integrity depend entirely on individual club owners/agents, not platform management[56][57]
- **No Platform Policing**: Developer (Mondraw Limited/PT.farm) does not actively police game integrity or ban wrongdoers at the platform level[58]

### 6. Security & Fairness
- **Account Security**: Standard app-store login flows (guest or Facebook), published privacy policy and terms of service, community guidelines[59][60]
- **Age Verification**: Requires users to be 18+ or 13+ with parental consent[61]
- **Platform-Level Security**: Minimal - no IP/GPS safeguards, no prohibition of collusion/botting in terms of use, no active policing by developer[62][63]
- **Club-Level Security**: Most security and anti-cheating measures managed by individual club owners/agents[64]
- **Anti-Tracking Design**: Purposely limits third-party tools - no HUDs or tracking software allowed[65]
- **Cheating Vulnerability**: System weaknesses theoretically allow single users to create multiple accounts[66]
- **Trust-Based Model**: Private games rely heavily on group trust rather than platform enforcement[67]
- **Reporting Tools**: Some reporting functionality available but limited platform action[68]

### 7. Best Suited For
- **Mobile-First Players**: Friends who primarily play from phones and want a quick, code-based way to start private poker tables[69][70]
- **Casual Groups**: Small clubs or social groups that value a lively, casual mobile interface over advanced desktop tools[71][72]
- **Variety Seekers**: Players who enjoy multiple poker variants (Hold'em, Omaha, OFC, Stud) plus Blackjack and Rummy in one app[73]
- **Social Players**: Users who prioritize social features, chat, emojis, and community over technical poker tools[74]
- **Trust-Based Communities**: Groups with established trust where platform-level security is less critical[75]
- **International Players**: Users worldwide as there are no geo-restrictions[76]

### 8. Quick Comparison Notes
- **vs. PPPoker & ClubGG**: More casual and mobile-centric with simpler game setup but fewer deep management tools and weaker platform-level security[77][78]
- **vs. Poker Now**: Trades the convenience of browser play for richer mobile UI and built-in social elements (chat, gestures, club features)[79][80]
- **vs. Desktop Poker Software**: More accessible for mobile users but lacks professional features, HUDs, and robust anti-cheating measures[81]
- **Unique Position**: Strongest in casual mobile poker with variety of games and social features, but weakest in platform security and desktop accessibility[82]
